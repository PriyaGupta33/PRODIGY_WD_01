High Energy Consumption



The infrastructure supporting the internet, such as data centers, servers, and networking equipment, consumes significant energy. This energy demand contributes to carbon emissions, impacting the environment. High energy consumption poses a challenge for sustainable development because it often relies on fossil fuels, leading to environmental damage like greenhouse gas emissions, air and water pollution, and resource depletion. This impacts climate change, degrades ecosystems, and uses finite resources. It can also create social and economic problems, such as energy inequality and higher costs for consumers.






E-Waste



The rapid pace of technology advancement leads to a shorter lifespan for electronic devices, resulting in increased electronic waste (e-waste). E-waste contains hazardous materials that pose risks to the environment and human health. E-waste (electronic waste) refers to discarded electronics like smartphones, laptops, and appliances. It poses significant environmental and health risks due to hazardous substances such as lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can contaminate air, soil, and water if not properly managed. E-waste also represents a loss of valuable resources like gold, silver, and copper.






Cybersecurity Risks



The increased reliance on the internet brings cybersecurity risks, including data breaches, identity theft, and cyberattacks. These risks can undermine trust and disrupt efforts toward sustainable development. In sustainable development, cyber security risks can arise from interconnected systems, reliance on digital infrastructure, and the potential for data breaches or cyber attacks targeting environmental or social initiatives. Protecting against these risks requires integrating robust cyber security measures into sustainable development strategies to safeguard data, critical infrastructure, and the overall integrity of projects.









Digital-Divide



This divide can hinder sustainable development by excluding certain groups from opportunities.The digital divide poses a significant challenge to sustainable development, as access to technology and digital literacy are increasingly crucial for economic growth, education, and social inclusion. Disparities in internet access, infrastructure, and technological skills create barriers for marginalized communities, hindering their ability to fully participate in the digital economy and benefit from advancements in areas such as healthcare and education. Bridging this gap requires targeted interventions to ensure equitable access to digital resources and opportunities for all, thereby fostering more inclusive and sustainable development outcomes.
